What is the technology?

The brain-machine interfaces are systems that create a direct communication pathway between the brain's electrical activity and external devices, such as computers or robotic limbs. It helps you have the intent to function the desire to change, move, control, or interact with something in your environment, directly from your brain activity. It bypasses nerves and muscles, allowing users to control computers, robotic limbs, or wheelchairs, and it can facilitate sensory feedback to the brain. Because of this, BCIs are being researched as a promising access technology for people with severe physical disabilities who have limited reliable control over their muscles and bodies.
